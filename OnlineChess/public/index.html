Chess Rules

The Objective

Chess is a two-player strategy game played on an 8x8 board. The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king — put it in a position where it is under attack and cannot escape. White moves first, then players alternate turns.

The Pieces & How They Move

Each player starts with 16 pieces: 1 King, 1 Queen, 2 Rooks, 2 Bishops, 2 Knights, and 8 Pawns.

King
Moves 1 square in any direction (up, down, left, right, or diagonal). The most important piece — if it's checkmated, you lose! The king can also castle (see Special Moves).
Queen
The most powerful piece. Moves any number of squares in any direction (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal). Cannot jump over other pieces.
Rook
Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically. Very powerful in open positions and endgames. Participates in castling.
Bishop
Moves any number of squares diagonally. Each bishop stays on its starting color (light or dark) for the entire game. The pair of bishops is powerful together.
Knight
Moves in an L-shape: 2 squares in one direction then 1 square perpendicular (or vice versa). The only piece that can jump over other pieces!
Pawn
Moves forward 1 square (or 2 from its starting position). Captures diagonally forward 1 square. Cannot move backward. Can be promoted when reaching the other side!

Special Moves

Castling

A special move involving the King and a Rook. The king moves 2 squares toward a rook, and that rook jumps to the other side of the king. This is the only move where two pieces move at once!

  • Kingside castling (O-O): King moves 2 squares right, rook moves to the left of the king
  • Queenside castling (O-O-O): King moves 2 squares left, rook moves to the right of the king

Requirements: Neither the king nor the rook has moved before. No pieces between them. The king is not in check, does not pass through check, and does not land in check.

En Passant

If a pawn advances 2 squares from its starting position and lands beside an enemy pawn, the enemy pawn can capture it "in passing" — as if it had only moved 1 square. This must be done immediately on the next move or the right is lost.

Pawn Promotion

When a pawn reaches the far end of the board (rank 8 for white, rank 1 for black), it must be promoted to a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight. Most players choose a Queen since it's the strongest piece.

Check, Checkmate & Stalemate

Check

When your king is under direct attack by an enemy piece, you are in check. You MUST get out of check on your next move. There are three ways:

  • Move the king to a safe square
  • Block the check by placing a piece between the attacker and your king
  • Capture the attacker

Checkmate

If your king is in check and there is no legal move to escape, it's checkmate — you lose! The game ends immediately. This is how most chess games are decided.

Stalemate

If it's your turn but you have no legal moves and your king is NOT in check, the game is a draw (tie). This is called stalemate. Be careful not to accidentally stalemate your opponent when you're winning!

Ways the Game Can End

  • Checkmate — One player wins by checkmating the opponent's king
  • Resignation — A player can resign (give up) at any time
  • Timeout — In timed games, running out of time loses the game
  • Stalemate — Draw if the player to move has no legal moves but isn't in check
  • Draw by Agreement — Both players agree to end the game as a draw
  • Insufficient Material — Draw when neither side has enough pieces to checkmate (e.g., King vs King)
  • Threefold Repetition — Draw if the same position occurs 3 times
  • 50-Move Rule — Draw if 50 moves pass with no pawn move or capture

Piece Values

While each piece's actual value depends on the position, these are the standard point values used to evaluate trades:

King∞Priceless — losing the king means losing the game

Quick Strategy Tips

  • Control the center — Pieces in the center control more squares and are more powerful
  • Develop your pieces — Get your knights and bishops out early. Don't move the same piece twice in the opening
  • Castle early — Protect your king by castling within the first 10 moves
  • Don't bring your queen out too early — She can be chased around and you'll lose time
  • Think about your opponent's threats — Before every move, ask "what is my opponent trying to do?"
  • Don't give up pieces for free — Always check if a square is defended before moving there
  • Connect your rooks — Once minor pieces are developed, connect your rooks on the back rank
  • Use all your pieces — A piece that isn't doing anything is a wasted piece

Help

Getting Started

  • Click Play Online to find or create a game
  • Set your name by clicking the edit button on the home screen
  • Choose a time control when creating a game, or play with no time limit

Playing a Game

  • Click a piece to select it, then click a highlighted square to move
  • Legal moves are shown as dots on the board
  • Use the Chat panel to message your opponent
  • You can Offer a Draw or Resign from the Actions panel

Your Profile

  • Your stats (wins, losses, draws) are saved locally in your browser
  • Change your avatar from the Profile page using the arrow buttons
  • Your rank updates automatically based on your win rate

Settings

  • Change the board theme in Settings (Classic, Ice, Forest, Royal)
  • Toggle sound effects, coordinates, and legal move highlights
  • Reset your stats if you want a fresh start

About

XS Chess

Version 1.0

A real-time online multiplayer chess game. Play against friends or strangers from around the world with customizable time controls.

Features

  • Real-time multiplayer with WebSocket connections
  • Multiple time controls (1, 3, 5, 10, 15, 30 minutes)
  • In-game chat with your opponent
  • Draw offers and resignation
  • Move history and captured pieces tracking
  • Customizable board themes
  • Local stats tracking with rank progression

Tech Stack

  • Frontend — Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS
  • Backend — Node.js + Express
  • Real-time — Socket.IO
  • Chess Engine — chess.js
